I have worked with studios such as Fluik Entertainment Inc, Corus Entertainment Group and Eastside Game Studio as a designer and front-end Unity engineer.  My passions cover nearly the entire scope of the gaming industry: Programming and engineering, 2D and 3D art, gameplay and level design, team development and.  On this blog I will be sharing project progress as well as programming practices and patterns that have helped me become successful.

I have two personal projects in active development:

My game project is a super-hard 3D mini-golf platformer built for touchscreens.  The purpose of this project is to grow myself as a gameplay and level designer as well as deepen my understanding of the features Unity provides (ShaderGraph, Addressables, Lighting, Collab, etc).  I will be sharing screenshots and video of progress and hope to share some of the successes and failures as well as generate player feedback to make the best game I can.

I am also working on a [turn-based encounter system](https://github.com/dsabatier/Turn-Based-Encounter) in C# for use in Unity and MonoGame for the purpose of developing a better understanding of SOLID principles, object-oriented design patterns and unit testing through practice and making mistakes.  I will be finding excuses to implement patterns found in books like [Head First Design Patterns](https://www.amazon.ca/Head-First-Design-Patterns-Brain-Friendly/dp/0596007124) and [Game Programming Patterns](https://gameprogrammingpatterns.com/).
