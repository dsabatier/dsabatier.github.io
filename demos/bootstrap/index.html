12-Column Grid System

Bootstrap's 12-column grid is a Flexbox-based system for building responsive layouts. The 12 units allow for easy division into halves (6), thirds (4), and quarters (3).

Grids provide structure: They use Containers (alignment), Rows (wrappers), and Columns (content).

You assign classes like .col-md-4 to elements to define how many of the 12 available units they occupy at specific screen breakpoints.
.col-md-4 means 4 units out of 12, or 1/3 of the available space. The md in the class name refers to the medium screen size breakpoint.

The available breakpoints are:

  • .col-sm-: 576px
  • .col-md-: 768px
  • .col-lg-: 992px
  • .col-xl-: 1200px
  • .col-xxl-: 1400px

For Example: .col-md-6 .col-lg-3 means 6 units out of 12, or 1/2 of the available space on medium screens and larger, and 3 units out of 12, or 1/4 of the available space on extra large screens and larger.

Containers are used to align content and provide a responsive width. All rows should be wrapped in a container.

Rows are used to wrap columns and provide a horizontal alignment. All columns should be wrapped in a row.

Columns are used to define the width of the content. All columns should be wrapped in a row.
